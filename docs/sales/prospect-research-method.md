# Prospect Research Method

Date: 2026-04-26

Purpose: find the first public business prospects to invite into the OctoBot Labs AI Workflow Risk Scorecard test.

## Who We Want First

The fastest validation group is not generic small businesses. It is teams already building, selling, or operating AI-enabled automations where credentials, tool permissions, write actions, approvals, and logs matter.

Priority 1:

- AI automation agencies and consultants using n8n, Make, Zapier, LangChain, CrewAI, OpenAI, Claude, or MCP.
- n8n and Make partners that mention AI workflows, production workflows, governance, monitoring, secure architecture, ERP/CRM/API integrations, or managed automation.
- Small product teams building MCP servers, agent tools, managed auth, agent identity, or agent runtime infrastructure.

Priority 2:

- Zapier/Make/n8n experts without explicit AI positioning, but with enterprise automation or complex integrations.
- AI/security vendors that are a strong market signal but may be too large for first-contact validation.

Avoid for now:

- Random personal LinkedIn profiles.
- Scraped personal emails.
- Agencies that only sell content spam, social posting bots, or generic "AI agency" offers without workflow depth.
- Large enterprises unless the outreach angle is partnership/research rather than direct sales.

## Qualification Signals

Use public pages only. A prospect becomes invite-worthy if at least two of these are visible:

- They mention AI workflows, AI agents, LLMs, MCP, or agentic systems.
- They mention n8n, Make, Zapier, Pipedream, LangChain, CrewAI, OpenAI, Claude, or similar orchestration/tooling.
- They build production workflows that touch CRM, ERP, finance, support, internal tools, Google Workspace, Slack, Salesforce, GitHub, Stripe, or databases.
- They mention governance, security, compliance, monitoring, auditability, managed hosting, or enterprise readiness.
- They serve SMBs, mid-market, agencies, SaaS, operations, IT, finance, legal, manufacturing, or regulated workflows.

## Outreach Rule

The first invite should be low-friction and research-oriented:

> We built a 3-minute AI Workflow Risk Scorecard for teams giving AI agents or automations real tool access. If you send back your score, we will reply with the first 1-2 fixes we would prioritize.

No paid pitch in the first message. The goal is proof of pain:

- Do they click?
- Do they complete the scorecard?
- Do they share a real workflow concern?
- Do they ask for a mini-audit?
- Do they ask for a version for their clients?

## Contact Policy

Use company contact forms, partner directory contact buttons, generic business inboxes published by the company, or official website contact pages. Do not scrape personal emails or enrich private data.

## Batch Design

Start with 100 public prospects:

- 70 automation agencies/consultancies.
- 20 MCP/agent tooling product teams.
- 10 security/identity/agent governance vendors for partnership intelligence.

Send in small waves of 10-20 so we can adjust the message before burning the whole list.

## Tracking Metrics

Minimum tracking fields:

- Segment.
- Source URL.
- Contact path.
- Invite angle.
- Contacted date.
- Reply status.
- Scorecard completed.
- Score band.
- Mini-audit requested.
- Notes.

Success threshold for this test:

- 100 researched prospects.
- 60 contacted.
- 6 replies.
- 3 completed scorecards.
- 1 paid or serious pilot conversation.
