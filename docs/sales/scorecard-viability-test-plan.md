# AI Workflow Risk Scorecard Viability Test

## Goal

Validate whether the market responds to a practical AI workflow risk diagnostic strongly enough to justify selling the audit offer.

## Asset

- Public scorecard: `https://octo-bot.io/scorecard.html`
- Primary CTA: request a mini-audit after receiving a score
- Follow-up offer: EUR 750 focused pilot audit

## Target Segments

Start with prospects already talking about AI agents or automation:

- AI automation agencies
- n8n, Make, Zapier, and workflow consultants
- SaaS teams publishing MCP servers or agent tools
- Founder-led B2B SaaS companies using internal AI workflows
- Small agencies shipping AI assistants for clients

## 14-Day Test

Send 100 targeted messages from the OctoBot Labs brand.

Track:

- Prospects contacted
- Scorecard clicks
- Scorecard completions known through replies or mini-audit requests
- Mini-audit requests
- Paid pilot conversations
- Paid pilot closes

## Pass Signals

Any one of these is enough to keep going:

- 20+ scorecard visits from 100 targeted prospects
- 5+ people reply with a score or ask for feedback
- 3+ mini-audit requests
- 1 paid EUR 750 pilot

## Weak Signals

If these happen, revise the pitch before building more:

- People click but do not complete
- People complete but do not request feedback
- Replies say the risk is interesting but not urgent
- Prospects ask for general AI strategy instead of workflow safety

## Kill Or Pivot Signals

If none of these happen after 200 well-targeted prospects:

- No serious replies
- No scorecard completions
- No mini-audit interest
- No language from prospects that mirrors the problem

Then pivot the wedge. Possible pivots:

- MCP readiness review for SaaS teams
- AI automation QA and monitoring
- n8n/Make workflow reliability audit
- AI workflow documentation and handover package
